What is an Adjective?

An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun by providing more information about its qualities, characteristics, or attributes.
Examples of Adjectives
  • Descriptive: blue, tall, delicious
  • Quantitative: three, many, few
  • Demonstrative: this, that, those
  • Possessive: my, your, their

Degrees of Comparison

Adjectives can change form to show different degrees of the quality they describe.
Positive Degree

The basic form of the adjective

Tall

"John is tall."

Comparative Degree

Used to compare two things

Taller

"John is taller than Mike."

Superlative Degree

Used to compare three or more things

Tallest

"John is the tallest in his class."

Forming Comparative and Superlative
Adjective Type Comparative Superlative Example
1-2 syllable adjectives + -er + -est tall → taller → tallest
Adjectives ending in -y -y + -ier -y + -iest happy → happier → happiest
3+ syllable adjectives more + adjective most + adjective beautiful → more beautiful → most beautiful
Irregular adjectives Special forms good → better → best
