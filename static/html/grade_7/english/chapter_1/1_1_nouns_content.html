What is a Noun?

A noun is fundamentally defined as a word or a name used to identify a person, a place, an object, an idea, and more.
Examples of Nouns
  • Person: boy, Alex, teacher
  • Place: room, school, London
  • Object: book, computer, iPhone
  • Idea: love, hope, anger

Types of Nouns

Common Nouns

General or everyday names for people, places, or things.

  • computer
  • school
  • teacher
Proper Nouns

Specific names that always begin with a capital letter.

  • iMac
  • GCF International Christian School
  • Teacher Adam

Count Nouns

Can be counted and have plural forms.

  • dog → dogs
  • book → books
  • house → houses
Non-Count Nouns

Cannot be counted and don't have plural forms.

  • water (not waters)
  • happiness
  • information

Concrete Nouns

Can be perceived by the senses.

  • apple (can see, touch, taste)
  • thunder (can hear)
  • flower (can see, smell, touch)
Abstract Nouns

Intangible concepts and feelings.

  • love
  • fear
  • justice

Functions of Nouns in a Sentence

1. Subject

The doer of the action or what is being described.

"The students went on a field trip."

2. Subject Complement

Completes the meaning after a linking verb.

"Andrew is a musician."

3. Direct Object

Receives the action directly.

"Matthew composed the song."

4. Indirect Object

Indicates to whom/for whom the action is done.

"Mark sent his mother a package."

5. Appositive

Gives details about another noun.

"My hometown, Manchester, is known for football."

6. Object of Preposition

Follows a preposition.

"John left his keys on the table."
