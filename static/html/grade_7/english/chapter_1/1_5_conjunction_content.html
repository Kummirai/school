What is a Conjunction?

A conjunction is a word that connects words, phrases, clauses, or sentences. Conjunctions help create complex, fluid sentences by showing relationships between ideas.
Examples of Conjunctions
  • Coordinating: and, but, or
  • Subordinating: because, although, while
  • Correlative: either...or, neither...nor

Conjunction Usage Rules

Proper use of conjunctions helps create clear, grammatically correct sentences.
Comma Rules
  • Coordinating conjunctions: Use a comma before when connecting two independent clauses
  • Subordinating conjunctions: Comma after dependent clause when it comes first
  • Correlative conjunctions: No comma between the pair
Parallel Structure

Elements connected by conjunctions should be grammatically equal:

  • Correct: "She likes hiking and swimming"
  • Incorrect: "She likes hiking and to swim"
