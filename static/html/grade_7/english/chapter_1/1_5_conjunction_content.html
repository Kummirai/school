What is a Pronoun?

A pronoun is defined as a word used to substitute nouns or a word used instead of a noun.
Example

"Missy lost her umbrella on her way home. She felt sad because it was a gift for her mother."

  • her/she refers to Missy
  • it refers to umbrella
Why Pronouns Matter

Without pronouns, sentences become repetitive:

"Missy lost Missy's umbrella on Missy's way home. Missy felt sad because the umbrella was a gift from Missy's mother."

Pronouns make language more efficient and natural.

Types of Pronouns

Refer to a specific person or thing and show gender, number, and person.

Subject vs. Object Pronouns
Type Singular Plural
Subject (does the action) I, you, he, she, it we, you, they
Object (receives action) me, you, him, her, it us, you, them
Subject Pronoun Examples
  • "He gave it to Soam."
  • "We agreed to hold a party."
Object Pronoun Examples
  • "He gave it to Soam."
  • "The principal called them."

Intensive Pronouns

Emphasize the subject (can be removed without changing meaning)

  • "The principal herself gave awards."
  • "I myself saw him copying."
Reflexive Pronouns

Show action reflects back on subject (necessary for meaning)

  • "I hurt myself."
  • "She looked at herself."
