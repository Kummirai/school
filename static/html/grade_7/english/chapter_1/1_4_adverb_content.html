What is an Adverb?

An adverb is a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. It typically answers questions like how, when, where, why, or to what extent.
Examples of Adverbs
  • Manner: quickly, slowly, beautifully
  • Time: now, later, yesterday
  • Place: here, there, everywhere
  • Degree: very, quite, extremely

Frequency Adverbs

Show how often something happens.

  • always, usually, often
  • sometimes, rarely, never
Focusing Adverbs

Limit or focus attention on specific information.

  • only, even, just
  • especially, particularly

Forming Adverbs

Many adverbs are formed from adjectives by adding -ly, but there are exceptions and special cases.
Regular Formation

Most adjectives form adverbs by adding -ly:

  • quick → quickly
  • happy → happily
  • beautiful → beautifully
Irregular Forms

Some adverbs have irregular forms or are the same as adjectives:

  • good → well
  • fast → fast
  • hard → hard
Special Cases
Adjective Ending Adverb Formation Example
-y Change y to i and add -ly happy → happily
-le Remove e and add -y gentle → gently
-ic Add -ally basic → basically
-ll Just add -y full → fully
