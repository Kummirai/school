Geometry of 2D Shapes: Similarity and Congruent Shapes

5.7 Similarity and congruent shapes

In geometry, we often compare shapes to see if they are the same or if they are scaled versions of each other. This leads to the concepts of similarity and congruence.

Similar Shapes

Similar shapes have the same shape but can be different sizes.

  • Their corresponding angles are equal.
  • Their corresponding sides are in proportion (meaning the ratio of corresponding side lengths is constant).
  • One shape is an enlargement or reduction of the other.

These two triangles are similar. One is an enlarged version of the other.

Think of photographs of the same object taken from the same angle but printed in different sizes. They are similar.

Congruent Shapes

Congruent shapes have exactly the same shape and the same size.

  • Their corresponding angles are equal.
  • Their corresponding sides are equal in length.
  • If you can superimpose one shape perfectly on top of the other (by sliding, rotating, or flipping), they are congruent.

These two triangles are congruent. They are identical copies.

Think of identical twins; they are congruent. Or two copies of the same page printed from a book.

Key Differences and Relationships

  • All congruent shapes are also similar. (If they are the exact same size and shape, they certainly have the same shape.)
  • Not all similar shapes are congruent. (Similar shapes can be different sizes.)

Activities to Understand Similarity and Congruence (Referencing Textbook Activity)

The textbook uses groups of quadrilaterals (Group A, B, C etc.) to illustrate these concepts.

  • Group A (e.g., blue shapes in textbook): These are described as having the "same form". They represent **similar shapes** (differing in size).
  • Group B (e.g., red shapes in textbook): These are described as having the "same form and the same size". They represent **congruent shapes**.
  • To check for congruence: Can you physically overlay one shape exactly onto another? If yes, they are congruent.
  • To check for similarity: Do they look like scaled versions of each other? Do their angles match?

Example:

Consider a small square with sides of 2cm and a large square with sides of 5cm.

  • Are they **similar**? Yes, all squares are similar because their angles are all \(90^\circ\) and their sides are proportional (e.g., large side / small side = 5/2 = 2.5, which is a constant ratio).
  • Are they **congruent**? No, because they are different sizes.

Consider two equilateral triangles, one with 3cm sides and another with 3cm sides.

  • Are they **similar**? Yes, all equilateral triangles are similar (all angles are \(60^\circ\)).
  • Are they **congruent**? Yes, because they are the same size and same shape.
