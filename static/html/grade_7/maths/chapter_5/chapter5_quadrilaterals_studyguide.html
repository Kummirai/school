Key Concepts

Quadrilateral: A four-sided polygon with four angles.

Sum of interior angles: Always 360° for any quadrilateral.

Types of Quadrilaterals

1. Parallelogram

  • Two pairs of parallel sides
  • Opposite sides equal in length
  • Opposite angles equal
  • Consecutive angles supplementary (add to 180°)
  • Diagonals bisect each other

2. Rectangle

  • All properties of a parallelogram
  • Four right angles (90°)
  • Diagonals are equal in length

3. Rhombus

  • All sides equal in length
  • Opposite sides parallel
  • Opposite angles equal
  • Diagonals bisect the angles

4. Square

  • All properties of parallelogram, rectangle, and rhombus
  • All sides equal
  • All angles 90°
  • Diagonals equal in length and bisect at 90°
  • Diagonals bisect angles (45° each)

5. Trapezium (Trapezoid)

  • Exactly one pair of parallel sides (bases)

6. Kite

  • Two distinct pairs of equal-length adjacent sides
  • One pair of opposite angles equal
  • One diagonal is perpendicular bisector of the other

Memory Tips

  • All quadrilaterals: Sum of angles = 360°
  • Parallelogram family: Rectangle, Rhombus, Square are all special parallelograms
  • Square: The "perfect" quadrilateral - has all the best properties
  • Kite vs Rhombus: A rhombus is a special kite with all sides equal
