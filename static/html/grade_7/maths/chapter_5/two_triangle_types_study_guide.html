Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 5

Geometry of 2D Shapes: Different Types of Triangles

5.2 Different types of triangles

Triangles are classified in two main ways: based on the lengths of their sides and based on the measures of their angles. Understanding these classifications is key to working with triangles.

Classification by Sides

  • Equilateral Triangle:
    • All three sides are equal in length.
    • All three angles are also equal, each measuring \(60^\circ\).

    Equilateral Triangle

  • Isosceles Triangle:
    • At least two sides are equal in length.
    • The angles opposite the equal sides (base angles) are also equal.

    Isosceles Triangle

  • Scalene Triangle:
    • All three sides have different lengths.
    • All three angles have different measures.

    Scalene Triangle

Classification by Angles

  • Right-angled Triangle:
    • Has one angle that measures exactly \(90^\circ\) (a right angle).
    • The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse, which is always the longest side.

    Right-angled Triangle

  • Acute-angled Triangle:
    • All three angles are acute (each measures less than \(90^\circ\)).

    Acute-angled Triangle

  • Obtuse-angled Triangle:
    • Has one angle that is obtuse (measures greater than \(90^\circ\) but less than \(180^\circ\)).
    • It can only have one obtuse angle.

    Obtuse-angled Triangle

Summary of Triangle Properties

  • The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is always \(180^\circ\).
  • An equilateral triangle is also an acute-angled triangle.
  • An isosceles triangle can be acute-angled, right-angled, or obtuse-angled.
  • A scalene triangle can also be acute-angled, right-angled, or obtuse-angled.
