Geometry of 2D Shapes: Quadrilaterals

5.6 Quadrilaterals

Quadrilaterals are a family of four-sided polygons. They come in various forms, each with unique properties. Understanding these properties helps in classifying them and solving related geometric problems.

General Properties of Quadrilaterals

  • A quadrilateral is a closed figure with four straight sides and four angles.
  • The sum of the interior angles of any quadrilateral is \(360^\circ\).

Types of Quadrilaterals

1. Parallelogram

A quadrilateral with two pairs of parallel sides.

Properties of a Parallelogram:

  • Opposite sides are parallel.
  • Opposite sides are equal in length.
  • Opposite angles are equal.
  • Consecutive (adjacent) angles are supplementary (add up to \(180^\circ\)).
  • Diagonals bisect each other (cut each other in half).

2. Rectangle

A parallelogram with all four angles equal to \(90^\circ\) (right angles).

Properties of a Rectangle:

  • All properties of a parallelogram.
  • All four angles are right angles (\(90^\circ\)).
  • Diagonals are equal in length.

3. Rhombus

A parallelogram with all four sides equal in length.

Properties of a Rhombus:

  • All properties of a parallelogram.
  • All four sides are equal in length.
  • Diagonals bisect each other at \(90^\circ\) (are perpendicular).
  • Diagonals bisect the angles of the rhombus.

4. Square

A quadrilateral that is both a rectangle and a rhombus. It has all properties of both.

Properties of a Square:

  • All properties of a parallelogram, rectangle, and rhombus.
  • All four sides are equal in length.
  • All four angles are right angles (\(90^\circ\)).
  • Diagonals are equal in length and bisect each other at \(90^\circ\).
  • Diagonals bisect the angles (\(45^\circ\) each).

5. Trapezium (or Trapezoid)

A quadrilateral with at least one pair of parallel sides.

Properties of a Trapezium:

  • Exactly one pair of parallel sides (called bases).

6. Kite

A quadrilateral with two distinct pairs of equal-length adjacent sides.

Properties of a Kite:

  • Two pairs of adjacent sides are equal in length.
  • One pair of opposite angles are equal (the angles between the unequal sides).
  • One diagonal is the perpendicular bisector of the other diagonal.
  • One diagonal bisects the angles at the vertices it connects.
