Study Guide: Types of Triangles

A triangle is a three-sided polygon with three angles. The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is always 180°.

Classification by Sides

1. Equilateral Triangle

  • All three sides are equal in length
  • All three angles are equal (each measures 60°)
  • Also known as a regular triangle
  • Always acute-angled

Equilateral Triangle

2. Isosceles Triangle

  • At least two sides are equal in length
  • The angles opposite the equal sides (base angles) are equal
  • Can be acute, right, or obtuse-angled

Isosceles Triangle

3. Scalene Triangle

  • All three sides have different lengths
  • All three angles have different measures
  • Can be acute, right, or obtuse-angled

Scalene Triangle

Classification by Angles

1. Right-angled Triangle

  • Has one angle that measures exactly 90° (a right angle)
  • The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse (longest side)
  • Follows the Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c² (where c is the hypotenuse)

Right-angled Triangle

2. Acute-angled Triangle

  • All three angles are acute (each measures less than 90°)
  • An equilateral triangle is always acute-angled

Acute-angled Triangle

3. Obtuse-angled Triangle

  • Has one angle that is obtuse (measures greater than 90° but less than 180°)
  • Can only have one obtuse angle

Obtuse-angled Triangle

Key Properties Summary

Property Description
Angle Sum The sum of interior angles is always 180°
Side Lengths The sum of any two sides must be greater than the third side
Equilateral Triangle All sides equal, all angles equal (60°), always acute-angled
Isosceles Triangle At least two equal sides, two equal angles, can be acute, right, or obtuse-angled
Scalene Triangle All sides unequal, all angles unequal, can be acute, right, or obtuse-angled

Note: A triangle can be classified both by its sides and by its angles. For example, a triangle can be a "right-angled isosceles triangle" (having a 90° angle and two equal sides).

Practice Questions

1. What type of triangle has all angles less than 90°?

Answer: Acute-angled triangle

2. If a triangle has sides of length 5cm, 5cm, and 7cm, what type is it?

Answer: Isosceles triangle (two equal sides)

3. Can a triangle have more than one obtuse angle? Why or why not?

Answer: No, because the sum of three angles in a triangle must be 180°. If one angle is obtuse (>90°), the other two must be acute to keep the total at 180°.
