Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 6

Fractions: Comparing Fractions

6.4 Comparing fractions

Comparing fractions means determining which fraction is larger, smaller, or if they are equal. This is an important skill when working with parts of a whole.

Comparing Fractions with the Same Denominator

When fractions have the same denominator, it's straightforward to compare them: the fraction with the larger numerator is the larger fraction.

Example: Compare \(\frac{3}{5}\) and \(\frac{2}{5}\).

\(\frac{3}{5}\)
>
\(\frac{2}{5}\)

Since 3 is greater than 2, \(\frac{3}{5}\) is greater than \(\frac{2}{5}\).

\[ \frac{3}{5} > \frac{2}{5} \]

Comparing Fractions with the Same Numerator

When fractions have the same numerator, the fraction with the smaller denominator is the larger fraction. This is because a smaller denominator means the whole has been divided into fewer, larger pieces.

Example: Compare \(\frac{1}{2}\) and \(\frac{1}{4}\).

\(\frac{1}{2}\)
>
\(\frac{1}{4}\)

One half is larger than one quarter.

\[ \frac{1}{2} > \frac{1}{4} \]

Comparing Fractions with Different Numerators and Denominators

This is the most common scenario. To compare such fractions, you need to find a common denominator (often the lowest common denominator, LCD) and then compare the new numerators.

Steps to Compare Fractions:

  1. Find the Lowest Common Denominator (LCD): This is the smallest common multiple of the denominators.
  2. Convert to Equivalent Fractions: Rewrite each fraction as an equivalent fraction with the LCD as the new denominator.
  3. Compare Numerators: Once the denominators are the same, compare the numerators as described above.

Example: Compare \(\frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{3}{4}\).

  1. Find LCD: The multiples of 3 are 3, 6, 9, 12, 15... The multiples of 4 are 4, 8, 12, 16... The LCD of 3 and 4 is 12.
  2. Convert to Equivalent Fractions:
    • For \(\frac{2}{3}\): Multiply numerator and denominator by 4 to get a denominator of 12. \[ \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2 \times 4}{3 \times 4} = \frac{8}{12} \]
    • For \(\frac{3}{4}\): Multiply numerator and denominator by 3 to get a denominator of 12. \[ \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3 \times 3}{4 \times 3} = \frac{9}{12} \]
  3. Compare Numerators: Now compare \(\frac{8}{12}\) and \(\frac{9}{12}\). Since \(8 < 9\), then \(\frac{8}{12} < \frac{9}{12}\).

Therefore, \(\frac{2}{3} < \frac{3}{4}\).

Alternative Method: Cross-Multiplication

This is a quicker method for comparing two fractions, especially useful in multiple-choice questions or for a quick check. Multiply the numerator of the first fraction by the denominator of the second, and the numerator of the second by the denominator of the first. Compare the products.

Example: Compare \(\frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{3}{4}\).

  • Multiply 2 (numerator of first) by 4 (denominator of second): \(2 \times 4 = 8\)
  • Multiply 3 (numerator of second) by 3 (denominator of first): \(3 \times 3 = 9\)

Since \(8 < 9\), then \(\frac{2}{3} < \frac{3}{4}\).
