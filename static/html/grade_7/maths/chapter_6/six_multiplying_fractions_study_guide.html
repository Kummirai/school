Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 6

Fractions: Multiplying Fractions

6.6 Multiplying fractions

Multiplying fractions is generally simpler than adding or subtracting them because you don't need a common denominator. You simply multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.

Multiplying Proper Fractions

To multiply two fractions, follow these steps:

  1. Multiply the numerators to get the new numerator.
  2. Multiply the denominators to get the new denominator.
  3. Simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

Example 1:

Calculate: \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4}\)

Multiply numerators: \(1 \times 3 = 3\)

Multiply denominators: \(2 \times 4 = 8\)

Result: \(\frac{3}{8}\)

\[ \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{1 \times 3}{2 \times 4} = \frac{3}{8} \]

Example 2:

Calculate: \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6}\)

Multiply numerators: \(2 \times 5 = 10\)

Multiply denominators: \(3 \times 6 = 18\)

Result: \(\frac{10}{18}\)

Simplify: Divide both by 2: \(\frac{10 \div 2}{18 \div 2} = \frac{5}{9}\)

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{10}{18} = \frac{5}{9} \]

Multiplying a Fraction by a Whole Number

To multiply a fraction by a whole number, first write the whole number as a fraction by placing it over 1. Then, proceed with the multiplication of two fractions.

Example:

Calculate: \(5 \times \frac{2}{7}\)

Write 5 as a fraction: \(\frac{5}{1}\)

Multiply: \(\frac{5}{1} \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{5 \times 2}{1 \times 7} = \frac{10}{7}\)

Convert to mixed number (optional, but good practice): \(\frac{10}{7} = 1 \frac{3}{7}\)

\[ 5 \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{5}{1} \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{10}{7} = 1 \frac{3}{7} \]

Multiplying Mixed Numbers

To multiply mixed numbers, it is usually easiest to convert them into improper fractions first. Then, multiply the improper fractions as usual.

Example:

Calculate: \(1 \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \frac{1}{3}\)

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions:

  • \(1 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{(1 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}\)
  • \(2 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{(2 \times 3) + 1}{3} = \frac{7}{3}\)

Multiply the improper fractions:

\[ \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{7}{3} = \frac{3 \times 7}{2 \times 3} = \frac{21}{6} \]

Simplify the result:

Divide both by 3: \(\frac{21 \div 3}{6 \div 3} = \frac{7}{2}\)

Convert back to a mixed number: \(\frac{7}{2} = 3 \frac{1}{2}\)

\[ 1 \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{7}{3} = \frac{21}{6} = \frac{7}{2} = 3 \frac{1}{2} \]

"Of" Means Multiply

In word problems, the word "of" often implies multiplication, especially when dealing with fractions of a quantity.

Example:

What is \(\frac{1}{3}\) of 9?

\[ \frac{1}{3} \times 9 = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{9}{1} = \frac{1 \times 9}{3 \times 1} = \frac{9}{3} = 3 \]

So, \(\frac{1}{3}\) of 9 is 3.
