Geometry of 2D Shapes: Parallel Lines and Transversals

5.5 Parallel lines and transversals

This section introduces important angle relationships formed when a line (called a transversal) intersects two parallel lines. These relationships are crucial for solving problems in geometry.

What are Parallel Lines?

Parallel lines are lines in a plane that are always the same distance apart and never meet, no matter how far they are extended. We use arrows on lines to indicate they are parallel.

Lines AB and CD are parallel.

What is a Transversal?

A transversal is a line that intersects two or more other lines (which may or may not be parallel).

Line XY is a transversal intersecting parallel lines AB and CD.

Angle Relationships Formed by Parallel Lines and a Transversal

When a transversal intersects two parallel lines, specific pairs of angles are formed that have special relationships:

1. Corresponding Angles (F-shape)

  • Corresponding angles are in the same relative position at each intersection.
  • They form an "F" shape.
  • Corresponding angles are equal.

Angles \(e\) and \(a\), \(f\) and \(b\), \(g\) and \(c\), \(h\) and \(d\) are corresponding angles.

2. Alternate Interior Angles (Z-shape)

  • Alternate interior angles are on opposite sides of the transversal and between the parallel lines.
  • They form a "Z" shape.
  • Alternate interior angles are equal.

Angles \(c\) and \(f\), and \(d\) and \(e\) are alternate interior angles.

3. Co-interior Angles (U-shape)

  • Co-interior angles (also called consecutive interior angles) are on the same side of the transversal and between the parallel lines.
  • They form a "U" shape (or "C" shape).
  • Co-interior angles add up to \(180^\circ\) (they are supplementary).

Angles \(c\) and \(e\), and \(d\) and \(f\) are co-interior angles. So, \(c + e = 180^\circ\) and \(d + f = 180^\circ\).

Applying these Relationships

These relationships are essential for finding unknown angles in diagrams involving parallel lines.

Example:

Given that line AB is parallel to line CD, and angle \(x = 55^\circ\). Find angles \(y\) and \(z\).

Find y and z.

Solution:
Angle \(y = 55^\circ\) (Corresponding angles, since AB || CD).
Angle \(x + z = 180^\circ\) (Co-interior angles, since AB || CD).
\(55^\circ + z = 180^\circ\)
\(z = 180^\circ - 55^\circ\)
\(z = 125^\circ\)
