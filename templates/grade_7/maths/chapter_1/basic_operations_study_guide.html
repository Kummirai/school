1.5 Basic Operations: Adding, Subtracting, Multiplying and Dividing

Mastering basic operations with whole numbers is crucial for all areas of mathematics. This section revises the concepts and properties of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

1.5.1 Addition and Subtraction

Addition is combining two or more numbers. The result of addition is called the sum.

Subtraction is finding the difference between two numbers. The result of subtraction is called the difference.

Properties of Addition:

  • Commutative Property: The order of numbers does not affect the sum. Example: \(5 + 3 = 3 + 5 = 8\).
  • Associative Property: The grouping of numbers does not affect the sum. Example: \((2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4) = 9\).
  • Identity Element: Adding zero to a number does not change the number. Zero is the additive identity. Example: \(7 + 0 = 7\).

Examples:

  • Calculate \(456 + 123\): 579
  • Calculate \(987 - 543\): 444

1.5.2 Multiplication

Multiplication is repeated addition. The result of multiplication is called the product.

Properties of Multiplication:

  • Commutative Property: The order of numbers does not affect the product. Example: \(5 \times 3 = 3 \times 5 = 15\).
  • Associative Property: The grouping of numbers does not affect the product. Example: \((2 \times 3) \times 4 = 2 \times (3 \times 4) = 24\).
  • Distributive Property: Multiplication distributes over addition (and subtraction). Example: \(5 \times (2 + 3) = (5 \times 2) + (5 \times 3) = 10 + 15 = 25\).
  • Identity Element: Multiplying a number by one does not change the number. One is the multiplicative identity. Example: \(7 \times 1 = 7\).
  • Zero Property: Multiplying any number by zero always results in zero. Example: \(7 \times 0 = 0\).

Examples:

  • Calculate \(25 \times 4\): 100
  • Calculate \(12 \times 30\): 360

1.5.3 Division

Division is sharing or grouping equally. The result of division is called the quotient.

It's important to remember that division by zero is undefined. Also, division is NOT commutative or associative.

Examples:

  • Calculate \(60 \div 5\): 12
  • Calculate \(125 \div 5\): 25
