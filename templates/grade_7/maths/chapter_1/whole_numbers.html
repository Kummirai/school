Working with Whole Numbers - Revision

1.1 Revision: Building and Breaking Down Numbers

In this section, we revise fundamental concepts of whole numbers, including writing sums as single numbers and understanding expanded notation. Remember, for this section, calculators are not to be used.

What is "Sum"?

The word "sum" is used to indicate two or more numbers that have to be added. The answer obtained when numbers are added is also called the sum. For example, 20 is the sum of 15 and 5.

Expanded Notation

When a number is written as a sum of units, tens, hundreds, thousands, etc., it is called the expanded notation. For example, 791 can be written as 700 + 90 + 1.

Examples:

  • Write 4000 + 800 + 60 + 5 as a single number: 4865
  • Write 8 thousands + 7 hundreds + 8 units as a single number: 8708
  • Write 8706 in expanded notation: 8000 + 700 + 6
