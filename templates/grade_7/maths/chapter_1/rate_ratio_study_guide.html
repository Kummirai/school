1.6 Problem Solving: Rate and Ratio

Rate and ratio are important concepts used to compare quantities. They help us understand relationships between different measurements or parts of a whole.

Understanding Ratio

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind. It shows how much of one quantity there is compared to another. Ratios can be written in several ways: using a colon (e.g., \(2:3\)), using the word "to" (e.g., 2 to 3), or as a fraction (e.g., \(\frac{2}{3}\)). Ratios should always be simplified to their simplest form.

Examples:

  • If there are 3 apples and 2 bananas, the ratio of apples to bananas is \(3:2\).
  • The ratio of boys to girls in a class of 10 boys and 15 girls is \(10:15\), which simplifies to \(2:3\).

Understanding Rate

A rate is a special type of ratio that compares two quantities with different units. Rates often describe how much of one thing there is per unit of another thing.

Examples:

  • Speed is a rate: kilometers per hour (km/h) or meters per second (m/s).
  • Cost per item is a rate: Rands per kilogram (R/kg) or dollars per litre ($/L).
  • Heartbeat is a rate: beats per minute (bpm).

A unit rate is a rate where the second quantity is 1 unit (e.g., 60 km/h, $5/kg).
