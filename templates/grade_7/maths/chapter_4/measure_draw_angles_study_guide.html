Construction of Geometric Figures: Measuring and Drawing Angles

4.3 Measuring and drawing angles

Measuring and drawing angles accurately are essential skills in geometry. This section focuses on the practical application of the protractor and other tools.

Measuring Angles with a Protractor

A protractor is the primary tool for measuring angles. It is usually a translucent plastic or metal semicircle marked with two scales, typically from \(0^\circ\) to \(180^\circ\) in opposite directions.

Steps for Measuring an Angle:

  1. Identify the Vertex: Locate the point where the two arms of the angle meet.
  2. Place the Protractor's Center: Position the small hole or mark at the center of the protractor precisely on the angle's vertex.
  3. Align one Arm with the Base Line: Rotate the protractor so that one of the angle's arms lines up exactly with the \(0^\circ\) line (the straight edge) of the protractor. Make sure the arm passes through the \(0^\circ\) mark on either the inner or outer scale.
  4. Read the Measurement: Follow the aligned arm up to the \(0^\circ\) mark. Then, read the measurement where the *other* arm crosses the protractor's curved scale. It is crucial to use the same scale that starts at \(0^\circ\) on the aligned arm.
    • If the first arm is aligned with the \(0^\circ\) on the right (inner scale), read the inner numbers.
    • If the first arm is aligned with the \(0^\circ\) on the left (outer scale), read the outer numbers.

Correct placement and reading of a protractor to measure an angle.

Drawing Angles with a Protractor

Drawing angles accurately is just as important as measuring them.

Steps for Drawing an Angle of a Specific Size:

  1. Draw an Arm and Mark the Vertex: Use a ruler to draw a straight line or ray. Mark a point at one end of this line/ray; this will be the vertex of your angle.
  2. Place the Protractor: Position the center hole of the protractor precisely on the vertex.
  3. Align the Base Line: Align the \(0^\circ\) line of the protractor with the arm you just drew.
  4. Mark the Desired Angle: Find the desired degree mark on the correct scale (inner or outer, depending on which side your arm is on). Make a small, clear pencil mark on your paper at this degree.
  5. Draw the Second Arm: Remove the protractor. Use your ruler to draw a straight line from the vertex through the pencil mark you just made. This line is the second arm of your angle. You can add an arc to indicate the angle you've drawn.

To measure the angle, use the outer scale of the protractor, starting from the 0° mark located at the bottom left

The number on the outer scale of the protractor that aligns with ray OA indicates the measurement of ∠AOC. Therefore, ∠AOC measures 143°.

Constructing Lines and Shapes with Given Angles

The ability to measure and draw angles allows us to construct more complex geometric figures, such as triangles and quadrilaterals, with specific properties.

For example, to draw a triangle with angles \(60^\circ\), \(60^\circ\), and \(60^\circ\):

  1. Draw a line segment of a chosen length.
  2. At one endpoint, use a protractor to draw a \(60^\circ\) angle.
  3. At the other endpoint, draw another \(60^\circ\) angle on the same side.
  4. Extend the arms of the two \(60^\circ\) angles until they meet. The third angle formed will also be \(60^\circ\).
