Construction of Geometric Figures: Working with Protractors

4.4 Working with protractors

This section provides practical exercises to solidify your understanding and skills in working with protractors, both for measuring existing angles and drawing new ones.

Measuring and Identifying Angle Types

It's important to be able to accurately measure angles and classify them correctly based on their degree measure.

Use your own protractor to measure these angles as part of the exercises.

Recall Angle Types:

  • Acute angle: \( < 90^\circ \)
  • Right angle: \( = 90^\circ \)
  • Obtuse angle: \( > 90^\circ \) and \( < 180^\circ \)
  • Straight angle: \( = 180^\circ \)
  • Reflex angle: \( > 180^\circ \) and \( < 360^\circ \)

Angles in Real-World Contexts

Angles are all around us, from the hands of a clock to the corners of a room. Being able to identify and estimate angles in real-world scenarios helps build a deeper understanding.

  • The angle formed by the minute and hour hand on a clock changes constantly.
  • Corners of square or rectangular objects (books, tables, walls) are typically right angles.
  • The opening of a pair of scissors or a door can represent various acute or obtuse angles.
