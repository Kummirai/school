Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 4

Construction of Geometric Figures: Using a Compass and a Protractor

4.8 Using a compass and a protractor to construct geometric figures

In this section, we will bring together the skills you've learned for using a protractor (for measuring and drawing angles) and a compass (for drawing circles and arcs) to construct more complex geometric figures. The combination of these tools allows for precise and versatile constructions.

Review of Tools and Their Uses

  • Protractor: Used for accurately measuring and drawing angles in degrees. Essential for constructing angles of specific sizes (e.g., \(60^\circ\), \(90^\circ\)).
  • Compass: Used for drawing circles and arcs, which are crucial for transferring lengths, bisecting lines and angles, and constructing perpendicular/parallel lines.
  • Ruler/Straightedge: Used for drawing straight lines and line segments, and for measuring lengths.

Constructing Regular Polygons (Example: Equilateral Triangle)

Combining the tools, you can construct regular polygons. Here's an example of constructing an equilateral triangle, where all sides are equal and all angles are \(60^\circ\).

  1. Draw a Base Line Segment: Use a ruler to draw a line segment of your desired side length. Label its endpoints A and B.
  2. Use Compass to Mark Third Vertex: Set your compass opening equal to the length of AB.
  3. Draw Arcs: Place the compass point on A and draw an arc above the line segment. Without changing the compass opening, place the compass point on B and draw another arc that intersects the first arc. Label the intersection point C.
  4. Complete the Triangle: Use your ruler to draw straight lines connecting A to C and B to C. You now have an equilateral triangle ABC.
  5. Verify Angles (Optional): Use your protractor to measure each angle. You should find that each angle is \(60^\circ\).

Construction of an equilateral triangle.

Constructing Angles (e.g., 60°, 90°, 120°) without direct protractor use for the angle itself

While a protractor measures angles, a compass can be used to construct specific angles precisely. For example, a \(60^\circ\) angle is the inherent angle of an equilateral triangle construction.

Constructing a \(90^\circ\) angle (Perpendicular) at the end of a line segment:

  1. Draw a Line Segment: Draw a line segment AB. You want to construct a \(90^\circ\) angle at point B. Extend line AB slightly past B.
  2. Draw an Arc: Place the compass point on B and draw an arc that intersects the line at two points (let's call them P and Q, one on each side of B).
  3. Draw Intersecting Arcs from P and Q: Open your compass to a radius greater than PB. Place the compass point on P and draw an arc above B. Without changing the compass opening, place the compass point on Q and draw another arc that intersects the first arc. Label this intersection point R.
  4. Draw the Perpendicular Line: Use your ruler to draw a straight line from B through R. This line BR will be perpendicular to AB, forming a \(90^\circ\) angle at B.

Construction of a \(90^\circ\) angle at a point on a line.

Practical Applications

These construction skills are vital in fields like architecture, engineering, and design for creating accurate diagrams and structures. They also help develop a deeper understanding of geometric properties and relationships.
