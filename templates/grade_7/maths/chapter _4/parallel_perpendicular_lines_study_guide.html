Construction of Geometric Figures: Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

4.5 Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

In this section, we will learn about two important types of lines: perpendicular lines and parallel lines, and how to construct them.

Perpendicular Lines

Perpendicular lines are lines that intersect (meet) each other at an angle of \(90^\circ\) (a right angle).

In the sketch, AB is perpendicular to DC. We write this as AB \(\perp\) DC[cite: 106].

Parallel Lines

Parallel lines are lines that never meet each other, no matter how far they are extended[cite: 107]. They are always an equal distance apart and have the same direction[cite: 107].

In the sketch, PQ is parallel to RS. We write this as PQ // RS[cite: 108]. The arrows on the middle of the lines also indicate that they are parallel[cite: 109].

Constructing Perpendicular and Parallel Lines

You can construct perpendicular and parallel lines using a protractor and a ruler[cite: 111].

Constructing a Perpendicular Line (Basic Method):

  1. Choose a point on the line where you want the perpendicular line to pass through.
  2. Place the protractor's origin exactly on this point and its base line exactly on top of the given line.
  3. Make a small, clear mark at the \(90^\circ\) mark on the protractor.
  4. Use a ruler to draw a straight line that passes through your chosen point on the original line and the \(90^\circ\) mark. This new line will be perpendicular to the original line.

Constructing a Parallel Line to a Given Line through a Given Point:

Let's say you want to draw a line that is parallel to line XY and passes through point A[cite: 112].

  1. Step 1: Draw a perpendicular line. Copy the line XY. Use your protractor to draw a line that goes through point A and is perpendicular (\(90^\circ\)) to line XY[cite: 113]. Label the point where this new line touches XY as C[cite: 114].

  2. Step 2: Measure the perpendicular distance. Measure the length of the line segment AC (the distance between point A and line XY)[cite: 115].

  3. Step 3: Mark another point at the same distance. Choose another point on line XY (e.g., further along). Draw another line perpendicular to XY at this new point. Measure and mark off the same length as AC on this new perpendicular line[cite: 117]. Let's call this new point B.

  4. Step 4: Draw the parallel line. Join point A with the new point B[cite: 119]. The line passing through A and B will be parallel to line XY[cite: 119].
