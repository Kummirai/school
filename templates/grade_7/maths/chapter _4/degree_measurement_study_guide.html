4.2 The degree: a unit for measuring angles

Just as we use units like centimeters or meters to measure length, we use a special unit called the **degree** to measure angles. Understanding degrees and how they relate to different types of angles is crucial.

Understanding Degrees

Imagine a full circle. It is divided into 360 equal parts. Each of these parts is called a **degree**, and its symbol is °.

A full rotation around a point is 360 degrees.

  • A full circle or a complete revolution is \(360^\circ\).
  • Half a circle or a straight angle is \(180^\circ\).
  • A quarter of a circle or a right angle is \(90^\circ\).

Estimating and Comparing Angles

Before using a protractor, it's helpful to be able to estimate angle sizes. You can compare angles to known angles like a right angle (\(90^\circ\)) or a straight angle (\(180^\circ\)).

Types of Angles based on Degrees:

  • Acute angle: An angle less than \(90^\circ\).
  • Right angle: An angle exactly equal to \(90^\circ\).
  • Obtuse angle: An angle greater than \(90^\circ\) but less than \(180^\circ\).
  • Straight angle: An angle exactly equal to \(180^\circ\). This looks like a straight line.
  • Reflex angle: An angle greater than \(180^\circ\) but less than \(360^\circ\).

Visual representation of acute, right, obtuse, straight, and reflex angles.

Using a Protractor

A protractor is a tool used to measure angles. Most protractors are semicircles marked with degrees from 0 to 180.

Steps to measure an angle with a protractor:

  1. Place the center hole of the protractor exactly on the vertex of the angle.
  2. Align the 0-degree line of the protractor with one arm of the angle.
  3. Read the measure of the angle where the other arm crosses the protractor's scale. Be careful to use the correct scale (inner or outer) depending on which 0-degree line you aligned with the arm.

Using a protractor to measure an angle.

Steps to draw an angle with a protractor:

  1. Draw a straight line or ray, which will be one arm of your angle. Mark a point on one end of this line/ray; this will be the vertex.
  2. Place the center hole of the protractor on the vertex.
  3. Align the 0-degree line of the protractor with the drawn arm.
  4. Find the desired degree mark on the protractor's scale and make a small mark with your pencil.
  5. Remove the protractor and use a ruler to draw a line from the vertex through the mark you just made. This forms the second arm of your angle.
