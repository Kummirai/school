2.3 The Order of Operations

When you have a mathematical expression with more than one operation, it's important to perform the operations in a specific order to get the correct answer. This order is commonly remembered by acronyms like **BODMAS** or **PEMDAS**.

BODMAS / PEMDAS

These acronyms help us remember the sequence of operations:

  • Brackets (or **P**arentheses)
  • Orders (or **E**xponents/Indices)
  • Division and **M**ultiplication (from left to right)
  • Addition and **S**ubtraction (from left to right)

It's crucial to remember that Division and Multiplication have the same priority, and should be performed from left to right as they appear in the expression. The same applies to Addition and Subtraction.

Examples:

  • Calculate \(5 + 3 \times 2\):
    • Multiplication first: \(3 \times 2 = 6\)
    • Then Addition: \(5 + 6 = 11\)
  • Calculate \((10 - 4) \div 2\):
    • Brackets first: \(10 - 4 = 6\)
    • Then Division: \(6 \div 2 = 3\)
  • Calculate \(4^2 + 3 \times (8 - 5)\):
    • Brackets first: \(8 - 5 = 3\)
    • Orders (Exponents) next: \(4^2 = 16\)
    • Multiplication next: \(3 \times 3 = 9\)
    • Finally, Addition: \(16 + 9 = 25\)
