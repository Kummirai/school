Exponents: Comparing Numbers in Exponential Form

2.5 Comparing Numbers in Exponential Form

To compare numbers written in exponential form, it is often helpful to calculate their actual values first. Once you have the values as standard numbers, you can easily compare them using greater than (\(>\)), less than (\(<\)), or equal to (\(=\)) signs.

How to Compare:

  1. Calculate the value: Expand the exponential notation and calculate the numerical value of each number.
  2. Compare the values: Once you have the standard numerical values, compare them directly.

Examples:

  • Compare \(2^3\) and \(3^2\):
    • \(2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8\)
    • \(3^2 = 3 \times 3 = 9\)
    • Since \(8 < 9\), we can say \(2^3 < 3^2\).
  • Compare \(10^3\) and \(100^1\):
    • \(10^3 = 10 \times 10 \times 10 = 1000\)
    • \(100^1 = 100\) (Any number to the power of 1 is itself)
    • Since \(1000 > 100\), we can say \(10^3 > 100^1\).
  • Compare \(4^2\) and \(2^4\):
    • \(4^2 = 4 \times 4 = 16\)
    • \(2^4 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 16\)
    • Since \(16 = 16\), we can say \(4^2 = 2^4\).
