2.6 Calculations: The Order of Operations

This section provides more practice with applying the order of operations (BODMAS/PEMDAS) to solve mathematical expressions. Remember, the key is to follow the sequence consistently to arrive at the correct answer.

Quick Recap of BODMAS/PEMDAS:

  • Brackets (or **P**arentheses)
  • Orders (or **E**xponents/Indices)
  • Division and **M**ultiplication (from left to right)
  • Addition and **S**ubtraction (from left to right)

Mastering these rules is crucial for complex calculations.

Examples (Review):

  • Calculate \(10 + 2 \times 5\):
    • Multiplication first: \(2 \times 5 = 10\)
    • Then Addition: \(10 + 10 = 20\)
  • Calculate \((15 - 5) \div 2 + 3^2\):
    • Brackets first: \(15 - 5 = 10\)
    • Orders (Exponents) next: \(3^2 = 9\)
    • Division next: \(10 \div 2 = 5\)
    • Finally, Addition: \(5 + 9 = 14\)
