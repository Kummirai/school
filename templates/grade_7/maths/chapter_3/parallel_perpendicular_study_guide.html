Geometry of Straight Lines: Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

3.2 Parallel and perpendicular lines

This section explores two important relationships between lines: parallelism and perpendicularity. Understanding these concepts is fundamental in geometry.

Parallel Lines

Parallel lines are two lines that maintain a constant distance from each other and will never meet, no matter how far they are extended in either direction[cite: 69, 100].

Lines AG and BH are parallel, meaning the distance between them is always the same.

Symbol:

The symbol used to indicate parallel lines is //.For example, we write: \(AG // BH\)[cite: 70].

Key characteristics:

  • Always maintain a constant distance apart[cite: 69].
  • Will never meet, even if extended indefinitely[cite: 100].
  • Can be line segments as well, if they maintain a constant distance and would not meet if extended[cite: 106].

Perpendicular Lines

Perpendicular lines are two lines that intersect (meet) at a right angle (90 degrees)[cite: 134].

Lines CD and KL are perpendicular, forming right angles at their intersection.

Symbol:

The symbol used to indicate perpendicular lines is \(\perp\).For example, we write: \(CD \perp KL\)[cite: 128].

Key characteristics:

  • Intersect (meet) each other[cite: 133].
  • Form right angles (90°) at their point of intersection[cite: 134].
  • When two lines are perpendicular, four right angles are formed at their intersection[cite: 133].
