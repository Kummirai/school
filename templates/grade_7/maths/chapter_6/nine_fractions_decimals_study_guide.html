Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 6

Fractions: Fractions in Decimal Notation

6.9 Fractions in decimal notation

Fractions and decimals are two different ways to represent parts of a whole. Decimals are essentially fractions with denominators that are powers of 10 (like 10, 100, 1000, etc.). Understanding how to convert between them is crucial.

Converting Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

Method 1: Division

Use long division to divide the numerator by the denominator.

Example 1:

Convert \(\frac{3}{4}\) to a decimal.

Divide 3 by 4:

\[ 3 \div 4 = 0.75 \]

So, \(\frac{3}{4} = 0.75\).

Example 2:

Convert \(\frac{1}{3}\) to a decimal.

Divide 1 by 3:

\[ 1 \div 3 = 0.333... \]

This is a repeating decimal, often written as \(0.\bar{3}\).

Method 2: Equivalent Fractions with Denominators of 10, 100, 1000...

If possible, convert the fraction to an equivalent fraction with a denominator that is a power of 10 (10, 100, 1000, etc.). Then, write the numerator as a decimal.

Example 3:

Convert \(\frac{2}{5}\) to a decimal.

Multiply the numerator and denominator by 2 to get a denominator of 10:

\[ \frac{2}{5} = \frac{2 \times 2}{5 \times 2} = \frac{4}{10} \]

Now, write \(\frac{4}{10}\) as a decimal: \(0.4\).

Example 4:

Convert \(\frac{7}{20}\) to a decimal.

Multiply the numerator and denominator by 5 to get a denominator of 100:

\[ \frac{7}{20} = \frac{7 \times 5}{20 \times 5} = \frac{35}{100} \]

Now, write \(\frac{35}{100}\) as a decimal: \(0.35\).

Converting Decimals to Fractions

To convert a decimal to a fraction, determine the place value of the last digit in the decimal. This will be your denominator (10, 100, 1000, etc.). The decimal digits form the numerator.

Example 5:

Convert \(0.6\) to a fraction.

The last digit (6) is in the tenths place. So the denominator is 10.

\[ 0.6 = \frac{6}{10} \]

Simplify the fraction: \(\frac{6}{10} = \frac{3}{5}\).

Example 6:

Convert \(0.125\) to a fraction.

The last digit (5) is in the thousandths place. So the denominator is 1000.

\[ 0.125 = \frac{125}{1000} \]

Simplify the fraction:

Divide by 5: \(\frac{125 \div 5}{1000 \div 5} = \frac{25}{200}\)

Divide by 25: \(\frac{25 \div 25}{200 \div 25} = \frac{1}{8}\)

So, \(0.125 = \frac{1}{8}\).

Converting Decimals with Whole Numbers (Mixed Decimals)

If a decimal has a whole number part, convert the decimal part to a fraction and then combine it with the whole number to form a mixed number.

Example 7:

Convert \(2.75\) to a mixed number.

The whole number part is 2.

Convert the decimal part \(0.75\) to a fraction: \(\frac{75}{100}\).

Simplify \(\frac{75}{100}\): Divide by 25: \(\frac{75 \div 25}{100 \div 25} = \frac{3}{4}\).

Combine: \(2.75 = 2 \frac{3}{4}\).
