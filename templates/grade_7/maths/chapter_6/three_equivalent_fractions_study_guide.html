Fractions: Equivalent Fractions

6.3 Equivalent fractions

Equivalent fractions are different fractions that represent the same value or the same portion of a whole. Even though their numerators and denominators are different, they cover the exact same amount.

Visualizing Equivalent Fractions

Consider a whole divided into parts. The same amount can be described using different fractions.

\(\frac{1}{2}\)
\(\frac{2}{4}\)
\(\frac{3}{6}\)

From the illustration above, you can see that \(\frac{1}{2}\), \(\frac{2}{4}\), and \(\frac{3}{6}\) all represent the same amount. Therefore, they are equivalent fractions.

\[\frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{3}{6}\]

Finding Equivalent Fractions

To find an equivalent fraction, you can multiply or divide both the numerator and the denominator by the same non-zero number. This is like multiplying the fraction by 1, because \(\frac{n}{n} = 1\).

Method 1: Multiplication

Multiply both the numerator and the denominator by the same number.

Example: Find an equivalent fraction for \(\frac{2}{3}\).

Multiply numerator and denominator by 2:

\[ \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} = \frac{4}{6} \]

So, \(\frac{2}{3}\) is equivalent to \(\frac{4}{6}\).

Multiply numerator and denominator by 5:

\[ \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2 \times 5}{3 \times 5} = \frac{10}{15} \]

So, \(\frac{2}{3}\) is also equivalent to \(\frac{10}{15}\).

Method 2: Division (Simplifying Fractions)

Divide both the numerator and the denominator by the same common factor. This process is often called simplifying or reducing fractions to their simplest form.

Example: Find an equivalent fraction for \(\frac{6}{8}\).

Divide numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor, which is 2:

\[ \frac{6}{8} = \frac{6 \div 2}{8 \div 2} = \frac{3}{4} \]

So, \(\frac{6}{8}\) is equivalent to \(\frac{3}{4}\). The fraction \(\frac{3}{4}\) is in its simplest form because 3 and 4 have no common factors other than 1.

Example: Simplify \(\frac{12}{18}\) to its simplest form.

Common factors of 12 and 18 are 2, 3, 6. The greatest common factor (GCF) is 6.

\[ \frac{12}{18} = \frac{12 \div 6}{18 \div 6} = \frac{2}{3} \]

So, \(\frac{12}{18}\) is equivalent to \(\frac{2}{3}\) in its simplest form.

Why are Equivalent Fractions Important?

Equivalent fractions are crucial for:

  • Comparing fractions: It's easier to compare fractions if they have the same denominator (which might require finding equivalent fractions).
  • Adding and subtracting fractions: Fractions must have a common denominator before they can be added or subtracted.
  • Simplifying answers: Often, fractional answers are expected in their simplest form.
