Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 6

Fractions: Dividing Fractions

6.7 Dividing fractions

Dividing fractions might seem tricky at first, but it's straightforward once you know the rule. The key is to use the **reciprocal** of the divisor and then multiply.

Understanding the Reciprocal

The reciprocal of a fraction is found by flipping the fraction upside down, meaning you swap the numerator and the denominator. For a whole number, place it over 1 and then flip it.

  • The reciprocal of \(\frac{2}{3}\) is \(\frac{3}{2}\).
  • The reciprocal of \(5\) (or \(\frac{5}{1}\)) is \(\frac{1}{5}\).

Dividing Proper Fractions

To divide one fraction by another, follow these steps:

  1. Keep the first fraction as it is.
  2. Change the division sign to a multiplication sign.
  3. Flip the second fraction (the divisor) to its reciprocal.
  4. Multiply the two fractions as you learned in the previous section.
  5. Simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

This method is often remembered as "Keep, Change, Flip" or "KCF".

Example 1:

Calculate: \(\frac{1}{2} \div \frac{3}{4}\)

1. Keep \(\frac{1}{2}\)

2. Change \(\div\) to \(\times\)

3. Flip \(\frac{3}{4}\) to \(\frac{4}{3}\)

4. Multiply:

\[ \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{1 \times 4}{2 \times 3} = \frac{4}{6} \]

5. Simplify: \(\frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}\)

\[ \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \]

Example 2:

Calculate: \(\frac{5}{8} \div \frac{1}{4}\)

\[ \frac{5}{8} \div \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{8} \times \frac{4}{1} = \frac{5 \times 4}{8 \times 1} = \frac{20}{8} \]

Simplify: Divide both by 4: \(\frac{20 \div 4}{8 \div 4} = \frac{5}{2}\)

Convert to mixed number: \(\frac{5}{2} = 2 \frac{1}{2}\)

\[ \frac{5}{8} \div \frac{1}{4} = \frac{20}{8} = \frac{5}{2} = 2 \frac{1}{2} \]

Dividing a Fraction by a Whole Number (or vice versa)

First, write the whole number as a fraction by placing it over 1. Then, proceed with the "Keep, Change, Flip" method.

Example 1 (Fraction by Whole Number):

Calculate: \(\frac{3}{5} \div 6\)

Write 6 as a fraction: \(\frac{6}{1}\)

\[ \frac{3}{5} \div \frac{6}{1} = \frac{3}{5} \times \frac{1}{6} = \frac{3 \times 1}{5 \times 6} = \frac{3}{30} \]

Simplify: Divide both by 3: \(\frac{3 \div 3}{30 \div 3} = \frac{1}{10}\)

\[ \frac{3}{5} \div 6 = \frac{1}{10} \]

Example 2 (Whole Number by Fraction):

Calculate: \(4 \div \frac{2}{3}\)

Write 4 as a fraction: \(\frac{4}{1}\)

\[ \frac{4}{1} \div \frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{1} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{4 \times 3}{1 \times 2} = \frac{12}{2} \]

Simplify: \(\frac{12}{2} = 6\)

\[ 4 \div \frac{2}{3} = 6 \]

Dividing Mixed Numbers

Just like with multiplication, the easiest way to divide mixed numbers is to convert them into improper fractions first. Then, apply the "Keep, Change, Flip" method.

Example:

Calculate: \(2 \frac{1}{4} \div 1 \frac{1}{2}\)

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions:

  • \(2 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(2 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{9}{4}\)
  • \(1 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{(1 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}\)

Now divide the improper fractions:

\[ \frac{9}{4} \div \frac{3}{2} = \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{9 \times 2}{4 \times 3} = \frac{18}{12} \]

Simplify the result:

Divide both by 6: \(\frac{18 \div 6}{12 \div 6} = \frac{3}{2}\)

Convert back to a mixed number: \(\frac{3}{2} = 1 \frac{1}{2}\)

\[ 2 \frac{1}{4} \div 1 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2} = 1 \frac{1}{2} \]
