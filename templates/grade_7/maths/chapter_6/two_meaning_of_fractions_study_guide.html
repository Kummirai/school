Grade 7 Mathematics: Chapter 6

Fractions: The Meaning of Fractions

6.2 The meaning of fractions

Fractions are a way of representing a part of a whole. They are used when we don't have a full unit but rather a portion of it. Fractions are written in the form \(\frac{numerator}{denominator}\).

Components of a Fraction

  • Numerator: The top number. It tells you how many parts you have.
  • Denominator: The bottom number. It tells you how many equal parts the whole has been divided into.

\[\frac{\text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}}\]

For example, in the fraction \(\frac{3}{4}\):

  • The numerator (3) tells you that you have 3 parts.
  • The denominator (4) tells you that the whole has been divided into 4 equal parts.

Fractions Representing Parts of a Whole

Imagine a whole object, like a pizza, a cake, or a line segment. When we divide it into equal parts, fractions help us describe how much of that whole we are talking about.

Example 1: Using a Rectangular Bar

Consider a rectangular bar divided into 5 equal parts. If 2 of these parts are shaded, the fraction representing the shaded portion is \(\frac{2}{5}\).

\(\frac{2}{5}\)
  • Numerator: 2 (number of shaded parts)
  • Denominator: 5 (total number of equal parts)

Example 2: Using a Circle

A circle divided into 8 equal slices. If 3 slices are eaten, then \(\frac{3}{8}\) of the pizza has been eaten.

The shaded part represents \(\frac{3}{8}\) of the circle.

Fractions Representing Parts of a Collection

Fractions can also represent a part of a group or collection of items.

Example:

There are 12 fruits in a basket: 5 apples, 4 oranges, and 3 bananas.

  • The fraction of apples is \(\frac{5}{12}\) (5 apples out of 12 total fruits).
  • The fraction of oranges is \(\frac{4}{12}\) (4 oranges out of 12 total fruits).
  • The fraction of bananas is \(\frac{3}{12}\) (3 bananas out of 12 total fruits).

Whole Numbers as Fractions

Any whole number can be written as a fraction with a denominator of 1. For example:

  • \(1 = \frac{1}{1}\)
  • \(5 = \frac{5}{1}\)
  • \(10 = \frac{10}{1}\)

Also, if the numerator and denominator are the same (and not zero), the fraction equals 1. For example, \(\frac{4}{4}\) means 4 out of 4 parts, which is the whole unit.

\[\frac{4}{4} = 1\]
