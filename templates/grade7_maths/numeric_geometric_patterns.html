11.1 Number Patterns in Sequences

What Comes Next?

What may the next three numbers in each of these sequences be?

4; 8; 12; 16; 20; ...
4; 8; 14; 22; 32; ...
4; 8; 16; 32; 64; ...
5; 7; 4; 8; 3; 9; 2; ...
Key Definition

Number Sequence: A set of numbers in a given order. In some cases each number in a sequence can be formed from the previous number by performing the same or a similar action. In such a case, we can say there is a pattern in the sequence.

Terms: The numbers in a sequence are called the terms of the sequence. Terms that follow one another are said to be consecutive.

Types of Patterns

Constant Difference

A sequence formed by repeatedly adding or subtracting the same number.

4 → 7 → 10 → 13 → 16
+3 +3 +3 +3

The difference between consecutive terms is constant.

Constant Ratio

A sequence formed by repeatedly multiplying or dividing by the same number.

5 → 10 → 20 → 40 → 80
×2 ×2 ×2 ×2

The ratio between consecutive terms is constant.

Pattern in Differences

Neither constant difference nor ratio, but a pattern in the differences.

2 → 5 → 10 → 17 → 26
+3 +5 +7 +9

Consecutive odd numbers are added each time.

Recursion

Recursion: The extension of a number sequence by repeatedly performing the same or similar action.

Recursive Rule: The rule that describes the relationship between consecutive terms.

The word "recur" means "to happen again".

Understanding Patterns

Summary

In all of the above cases it was possible to extend the sequence by:

  • Repeatedly adding or subtracting a number to get the next term, OR
  • Repeatedly multiplying or dividing by a number to get the next term, OR
  • Adding different numbers according to some pattern to get the next term
